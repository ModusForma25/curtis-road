## 1. Reading the brief — what ACCIONA is really asking for

The commercial attachment tells you as much as the requirements list. ACCIONA is bidding
under an **Incentivised Design Services Deed** with **painshare/gainshare**, and **KRAs**
for *materials reduction, early delivery of critical packages, and sustainability/innovation*.
That means they are not buying landscape drawings — they want a design partner who will:

- **Design to reduce** materials, carbon and capital cost (buildability-led design that feeds their gainshare and KRAs);
- **Deliver early and integrate** cleanly with the multidisciplinary tender team (they carry the integration risk); and
- **Help them WIN the head contract.** On a DIT level-crossing-removal, the public realm is the *visible, community-facing, politically salient* part of the scheme. Great place-making is how ACCIONA differentiates its tender to DIT and the Playford community.

So we are really writing to **two audiences at once**: ACCIONA (who selects us) and — through
ACCIONA's tender — **DIT and the Playford community**. Our EOI should show we understand we
are helping ACCIONA win, not simply servicing a scope.

**Read the fine print:** requirement 2 asks for *"a reduction in overall biodiversity impact
**compared with the concept design**."* That is an explicit invitation to **improve on DIT's
published concept** — i.e. demonstrate measurable biodiversity **net-gain/uplift**. It is the
clearest signal in the brief that they want design ideas, not methodology.

### The bigger picture — why this is now DIT's project (and who's watching)

Curtis Road is legally a **City of Playford council road**. It has become a **$250M, 50:50
Commonwealth–State project** (Albanese + Malinauskas Labor governments, ~$125M each; Playford
$3.4M) delivered by **DIT** — the state stepping onto a council road to remove a crossing the
local council could not fund alone. That shift changes what the project is *for*, and who is
watching. Showing we understand it signals we're a strategic partner, not just a drafting service.

- **It's a housing project as much as a transport one.** The northern suburbs are the centrepiece
  of the **Malinauskas Government's Housing Roadmap** — projected to take **~40% of Greater
  Adelaide's growth** over 30 years (Angle Vale, Riverlea, Concordia, Roseworthy). Curtis Road
  unlocks that growth front. The public realm is how "growth" reads as *liveable community*, not
  just more cars and houses.
- **Public money on a public stage.** ~$246.6M of Commonwealth/State money on a council road means
  the spend must visibly deliver *public value* — and the community-facing landscape and place
  outcomes are the most visible proof. DIT now owns that design-quality reputation.
- **Two governments want a legacy.** Federal + State Labor co-funding in a fast-growing northern
  heartland; safety (21,000 vehicles/day, booms down up to 15 min/hr), **~425 FTE jobs/yr**,
  congestion relief and community benefit are all politically valuable. Design quality is not
  optional dressing — it's the story government wants to tell.
- **DIT delivery = SA Government design expectations.** Government ownership brings DIT/SA standards
  and processes — likely design review (**ODASA / Government Architect**), **Kaurna/reconciliation**
  commitments, **Green Adelaide** canopy and WSUD policy, and genuine community engagement. ACCIONA
  needs a consultant fluent in DIT's world — which we are (T2D with DIT, permanent SA presence).

**What this means for our EOI.** ACCIONA isn't only buying landscape — it's buying help to deliver
a politically visible, housing-critical, publicly-scrutinised project that DIT and two governments
need to be *proud* of. Our line: *the landscape and urban design is the part that proves the public
investment built a community, not just a road — we design so DIT can stand under this bridge and
point to it.*

> **Tone caveat (important).** In the document be politically **astute, not partisan** — align to
> the **Housing Roadmap, public value, safety, jobs and community legacy**, not to any party. And
> do **not** criticise City of Playford's past under-investment (even though the record invites it)
> — Playford is a stakeholder we need on side. Frame the state's role as *partnership and support*,
> never as rescue.

## 2. Our positioning — three win themes to thread through every page

Pick a small number of themes and repeat them (headline, imagery, captions) so the document
reads as one argument, not seven disconnected sections.

1. **"Left-over to landmark."** The space *under and beside* an overpass is usually residual —
   fenced, dark, unloved. We make it the community's front door: safe, shaded, activated,
   owned. This is the single idea that most differentiates a landscape/UD response on a
   grade-separation.
2. **"Designed to regenerate."** Biodiversity net-gain, decarbonisation and circular materials
   are *designed into* the landscape and urban realm and **measured against the concept** —
   feeding directly into ACCIONA's KRAs and gainshare.
3. **"An Adelaide answer, informed by hard-won lessons."** **Modus Forma** — a Melbourne
   architecture practice specialising in **transport infrastructure** — brings lessons from the
   country's most mature **level-crossing-removal** program, *offered to be adapted to Adelaide,
   not imposed on it* (see the tone note below). We already have **SA runs on the board** — MF was
   **design architect on Torrens to Darlington**, delivered with Kaurna cultural leader **Karl
   Telfer**, whom **TCL has also worked with extensively** and whom we propose as **Aboriginal
   urban design consultant**. Crucially, **this is a team physically in Adelaide**: TCL leads the
   **soft landscape from its Adelaide office**, and MF has a **permanent staff member based in
   Adelaide** — not a fly-in. Plus MF's in-house **digital/parametric design & visualisation** (the
   concept model is ours). The result: national depth, an Adelaide-specific, Kaurna-led answer, and
   a team already known and present in SA.

> **Tone note — read before writing a word about Melbourne.** Adelaide is proud of its city and
> reacts badly to "Melbourne knows best." Never present Melbourne's LXR as the template Adelaide
> should copy. Frame it as **experience that de-risks Adelaide's own approach**: *"we've seen what
> works and what fails across dozens of crossings, so we can help Curtis Road avoid the pitfalls
> and do it the Adelaide way."* Every Melbourne lesson must arrive attached to **how it adapts to
> Adelaide's specific context** — Kaurna Country, the Adelaide Plains climate, Playford's
> community, SA materials and character. Lead with the Adelaide-specific solution; let the
> experience sit quietly underneath it as credibility, not as a lecture.

## 3. The differentiator — project analysis + design ideas to seed

The user's brief: *we try to show project analysis and design ideas beyond the standard EOI.*
Below is a bank of **site-specific** ideas to develop into 3–4 headline "design moves." Each is
grounded in Curtis Road / Munno Para — not portable to any project.

- **Structure-as-architecture, lessons adapted for Adelaide (Modus Forma).** As a transport-
  infrastructure architecture practice, MF designs the **overpass itself as a piece of
  architecture** — not just a span to be dressed. We bring hard-won lessons from Australia's most
  mature LXR program *as a way to de-risk Curtis Road, not a template to copy* — every lesson
  translated into **what it means for Adelaide's specific context** (Kaurna Country, the Plains
  climate, Playford, SA character and materials). Few landscape-led teams can shape the bridge; we
  lead with it — humbly, and Adelaide-first.
- **Country-led narrative with Karl Telfer (Kaurna).** Organise the whole scheme around one idea
  drawn from Country — the story of *water, woodland (wirra) and movement* across the Adelaide
  Plains — developed *with* Kaurna cultural leader **Karl Telfer** (our proposed Aboriginal urban
  design consultant, with whom MF worked as design architect on **Torrens to Darlington** and TCL
  extensively), not applied as decoration. Gives DIT a defensible cultural-design spine and satisfies SA Gov's
  design-with-Country expectations.
- **"The room under the bridge."** The Charlotte St–Alawoona Rd local connection and community
  space sit *beneath* the structure. Treat the undercroft as a designed **room**, not residual
  space: sculptural lighting, CPTED/passive surveillance, weather- and sun-shelter, court/play/
  gathering, robust materials. Directly proves win theme #1.
- **Greenway as spine.** Position the **Gawler Greenway** connection to Smithfield and Munno
  Para stations as the organising movement network — a continuous, green, safe, legible active-
  transport spine, with the **wombat crossings** designed as *place moments*, not just markings.
- **Cool corridor / canopy-first.** Munno Para is a hot, exposed, fast-growing outer suburb.
  Lead with an **urban-heat and shade strategy** — canopy targets, cool routes, species chosen
  for a warming, drying climate — so the paths are usable in an Adelaide summer.
- **Treat the overpass as a catchment (WSUD).** Harvest and clean stormwater off the deck and
  approaches to **irrigate the landscape that greens the corridor**; raingardens double as
  amenity and biodiversity. Water security de-risks the planting's establishment.
- **Biodiversity net-gain, measured.** Reinstate locally indigenous **grey box grassy woodland /
  native grassland** understorey of the Adelaide Plains; stitch habitat along the greenway;
  present a simple **canopy / habitat / species uplift metric vs the concept design** (answers
  requirement 2 head-on).
- **Social legacy for Playford.** One of SA's youngest, fastest-growing and most disadvantaged
  LGAs. Design for youth and safety; build legacy through **local jobs** — community
  propagation/nursery, First Nations enterprise in planting and maintenance, skills pathways.
- **Design-to-reduce (materials & carbon).** Cut hardscape, specify recycled/low-carbon and
  **site-won** materials, and shape retaining/embankment approaches to minimise concrete —
  explicitly linking our design decisions to ACCIONA's **materials-reduction KRA and gainshare**.

> **Our unfair advantage — the interactive concept model.** We have already built a working,
> parametric **3D concept model** of the Curtis Road scheme (alignment-driven from real survey/
> OSM data, with structure options, undercroft, shared-use paths, throw screens and station
> context). Use it three ways: **(a)** render stills as the hero images through the A3; **(b)** a
> **QR code** to the live model so ACCIONA can explore it; **(c)** drive it **live** in the
> 30-minute presentation. Almost no EOI arrives with a working model of the client's own scheme —
> it is concrete proof of "analysis and design ideas beyond the standard EOI."

## 5. Appendix checklist (excluded from the 10-page limit)

ACCIONA **will not select** a consultant until Attachment A is agreed and a tender-services cost
estimate is provided — so the appendix is a gating item, not an afterthought. Start the
commercial/insurance review **now**, in parallel with writing.

- [ ] **Hourly rates** — tender design / detailed design / construction-phase services; **tender-rate discount**; **expected % recovery on award**.
- [ ] **Cost estimate for tender services** (based on current scope knowledge + recent tenders).
- [ ] **Indicative list of tender advice notes** for the services.
- [ ] **Attachment A response** — accept, or propose departures in **track changes**. Flag for legal/commercial review before committing:
  - PI insurance: **$5M** (Tender Services Deed) / greater of **$20M or 50% of design fee** (IDSD), **10-yr run-off**, no access to any project-specific PI policy — check MF *and* TCL cover levels.
  - **Fit-for-purpose** warranty passed down from the Head Contract (not diluted to "reasonable skill and care").
  - **Painshare/gainshare** percentages and caps (negotiable — agree our exposure).
  - General liability caps ($5M TSD / greater of $20M or 50% fee IDSD); LinkedSite timesheets; 60/40 fee split.
- [ ] **CVs** for key team + steering committee — standard consultant format, as a separate appendix.
