**The teaming idea in one line:** *Modus Forma — a Melbourne transport-infrastructure
architecture practice — shapes the hard infrastructure so it gives, not takes, space, light
and connection; TCL, from Adelaide, turns the space it makes into a living, loved landscape.*
The proposal's differentiator is that **one team controls both sides of the interface** — the
deck soffit, the screens, the embankment, the undercroft — where most bids have a landscape
architect designing *around* a bridge someone else fixed. MF brings **lessons from Australia's
most mature LXR program — adapted to Adelaide, not imposed on it** — plus **SA delivery as design
architect on Torrens to Darlington** with Kaurna leader **Karl Telfer** (our proposed Aboriginal
urban design consultant, also a long-standing TCL collaborator). This is a team **in Adelaide**:
TCL leads the soft landscape from its **Adelaide office** and MF has a **permanent staff member in
Adelaide**. National depth; an Adelaide-specific, Kaurna-led answer; a local team in the room.

> **Tone rule for every Melbourne reference.** Adelaide is proud and dislikes being told Melbourne
> knows best. Never position Melbourne's LXR as the template. Frame it as *experience that de-risks
> Adelaide's own approach*, always paired with **how it adapts to Adelaide's context** (Kaurna
> Country, Plains climate, Playford, SA character). Lead with the Adelaide solution; let the
> experience sit underneath as quiet credibility.

# Part A — The design moves (deep dive)

Four moves. Each states the **issue**, the **move**, the **Modus Forma (hard) role**, the
**TCL (soft) role**, and the **measure vs the concept design** (which answers requirement 2
directly). Pick 3–4 as the spine of the document and repeat them in headers and captions.

## Move 1 — The Room Under the Bridge  *(flagship: hard + soft interface)*

**Issue.** On a grade separation the space *beneath and beside* the deck is usually residual —
dark, fenced, hostile, drainage-only. Yet at Curtis Road it is exactly where the concept puts
the **community space and the Charlotte St–Alawoona Rd local connection**. If it fails, the
whole "community landmark" story fails; if it works, it is the scheme's signature.

**The move.** Design the undercroft as a genuine **room** — with a ceiling, walls, light and a
floor — rather than leftover space treated after the fact.

- **Modus Forma (hard):** set the **span and pier arrangement to open sightlines** through the
  undercroft (fewer, better-placed piers; longer clear span over the community space so it
  doesn't read as a tunnel); design the **deck soffit as a considered ceiling** with integrated,
  vandal-resistant lighting and acoustic treatment; detail the **throw screens and F-type
  barriers** for amenity and **CPTED/passive surveillance** (transparency, no entrapment
  points), not just compliance; choose **embankment vs MSE wall** on the approaches to hold
  light and openness where people gather.
- **TCL (soft):** the floor and life of the room — robust shade-tolerant/structural planting,
  **play and gathering** (courts, youth space, shelter), materials and furniture that take hard
  use, and a landscape that reads as *cared for* to signal safety.
- **Interface (why one team matters):** soffit height, pier spacing, screen transparency and
  lighting are *hard* decisions that make or break the *soft* outcome. We resolve them together
  from day one — and can test them live in the concept model.

**Measure vs concept:** lux levels and % passively-surveilled area in the undercroft; usable
sheltered community m²; sightline lengths — all improved against the concept baseline.

## Move 2 — The Greenway Spine  *(connection over severance)*

**Issue.** The scheme's public value is **connection**: joining the **Gawler Greenway** to
**Smithfield** and **Munno Para** stations with **shared-use paths both sides** and **wombat
crossings**. Done as engineering-minimums it becomes a fast, hot, unsafe path nobody walks.

**The move.** Treat the active-transport network as a continuous **green spine** with the
crossings designed as *place moments*.

- **Modus Forma (hard):** shared-use path **structure, grades and ramp geometry** (comfortable
  gradients, generous widths), **wombat crossing** design, safety separation from the
  carriageway, **wayfinding armature** and lighting; the structural moves that let the path stay
  green and continuous across the corridor.
- **TCL (soft):** the **canopy and understorey** along the spine, rest/meeting nodes, the
  crossings as designed thresholds, legibility and comfort.
- **Interface:** where the path meets the structure, the station approaches and the greenway —
  the joints are hard/soft and we own both.

**Measure vs concept:** continuous shaded path length; crossing comfort/wait times; network
legibility; % of route with canopy cover at maturity.

## Move 3 — Design-to-Reduce  *(the hard-infrastructure carbon lever — our KRA gift to ACCIONA)*

**Issue.** ACCIONA's Incentivised Design Services Deed rewards **materials reduction, early
package delivery and innovation (KRAs + gainshare)**. The **biggest carbon and cost lever on
this project is the hard infrastructure itself** — concrete in the deck, piers, barriers and
retaining walls — which most landscape-led teams cannot touch. **We can.**

**The move.** Shape the structural form to strip embodied carbon and cost out of the scheme —
driving the optioneering *with ACCIONA's engineers* — and hand ACCIONA evidence for their gainshare.

- **Modus Forma (hard):** as infrastructure architects, **drive the form and structural
  optioneering** — structure type and span arrangement (e.g. steel network arch vs Super-T vs plate
  girder trade-offs), **span/pier rationalisation** to minimise concrete, **MSE embankment vs
  retaining wall** to cut wall area and material, maximising **site-won fill**, and pushing
  **low-carbon concrete and recycled content** — all developed collaboratively with the engineering
  team. Our **interactive concept model already computes retaining-wall area, fill volumes and span
  arrangements live** — a ready-made optioneering tool for exactly this conversation.
- **TCL (soft):** parallel reduction in hardscape (less paving, more planting), recycled/site-won
  materials in the landscape, and **carbon drawdown through canopy and soils**.
- **Interface:** early-package, buildability-led decisions that need hard + soft agreed together —
  which is what the incentivised model is built to reward.

**Measure vs concept:** embodied-carbon (kgCO₂e) and material-quantity reduction vs the concept
design; concrete/steel tonnes saved; % site-won material — presented as a KRA-ready panel.

## Move 4 — Regenerate the Plains  *(measured biodiversity net-gain + cool corridor)*

**Issue.** Requirement 2 asks for **reduced biodiversity impact *compared with the concept
design*** — an explicit invitation to *improve* on DIT's concept — in a hot, exposed, fast-growing
outer suburb (Munno Para / City of Playford) on the Adelaide Plains, on **Kaurna Country**.

**The move.** Turn the corridor from grey infrastructure into a **regenerating landscape** with a
number attached.

- **TCL (soft) + Karl Telfer (Kaurna):** reinstate locally indigenous **grey box grassy woodland /
  native grassland** understorey; stitch **habitat along the greenway**; a **canopy-first "cool
  corridor"** heat strategy (shade targets, species for a warming/drying climate); a **Kaurna
  Country-led** planting and narrative developed *with* **Karl Telfer** (Aboriginal urban design
  consultant, shared MF/TCL relationship from Torrens to Darlington); **social legacy** via
  community propagation/nursery and First Nations enterprise in planting and maintenance.
- **Modus Forma (hard):** treat the **overpass as a catchment** — capture and clean deck/approach
  stormwater to **irrigate the very landscape that greens the corridor** (water security de-risks
  establishment); WSUD infrastructure, raingardens as amenity, and structural provision for soil
  volume and tree pits within the hard works.
- **Interface:** the water and soil that the soft landscape needs are delivered by the hard works —
  designed together.

**Measure vs concept:** canopy cover %, habitat area, indigenous species count and stormwater
treated — each quantified as **net-gain vs the concept design**.

  Adelaide canopy and WSUD, real community engagement. We work fluently in DIT's world and hold
